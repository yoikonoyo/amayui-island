Explore Amayui Island

The coastal road has a cycling path and gentle ups and downs. Cycling is the best way to explore the island.
It takes about 4 hours to go around. Enjoy the island's leisurely pace and the sea breeze.
There are no shops or vending machines along the way, so please bring drinks and snacks with you.

Amayui Shrine

Designated as a Tokyo cultural property in 1959. The deity is Amanohime. It is revered as one of the gods who descended with the meteor shower.

Amayui Lighthouse

First lit on March 11, 1902. The current concrete structure was rebuilt in 1950. Standing 34m tall, it beautifully complements the calm coastline and blue sky.

Nature Trail

Enjoy scenery, seasonal scents, bird and insect sounds. Sunlight filtering through trees, small creatures, and unexpected encounters with protected species are highlights.

Sky Gate

A 3 km long limestone cave. Though details are unknown, the mystical space illuminated by dim light evokes the path to the sky believed by ancient people.

Morning Glory Field

In summer, the west Nishinohama coast is covered with vibrant purple morning glories, captivating visitors with the sea breeze and gentle floral scent.

Local Products Shop

Experience DIY renovations in old houses. The shop offers local products and a cafe, providing a perfect rest stop during your tour.
